What Is Service Oriented Architecture?

In Summary...

It's not about It's about
a programming language or network protocol an architecture for designing systems
large applications discrete business processes
starting over reuse
only "web services" web services as a means to an end
proprietary systems standards and open protocols

It's About an Architecture for Designing Systems

  • Pattern or philosophy for creating, enhancing and leveraging software components
    • Programming language-agnostic
    • ... even better, an equalizer

It's About Discrete Business Processes

  • Ignoring the "how", focus on the "what"
  • In newspaper production terms:
    • news reporting
    • selling advertisement space
    • managing subscriptions
    • editing, layout, and printing

It's About Reuse

    • Orchestrating business processes
    • under the direction of a supervisor
  • Using an "adapter" when necessary around legacy applications

It's About Web Services as a Means to an End

  • SOA is to blueprints as web services is to the plumbing
  • Use of web services demands a clean, well-defined interface between applications
  • SOA combines that clean, well-defined interface with business logic plus data to achieve a business function

It's About Standards and Open Protocols

  • Agreement about the plumbing

Why Should I Care?

'The ILS Market Implodes! Film at 11...'

  • Marketplace consolidation along with new market entries
    • (Clayton Christensen's Theory of Disruptive Innovation at work)
  • Monolithic "do everything" applications
    • (After all, isn't that why we call them "Integrated")
  • Dueling "us too" press releases
    • (a.k.a. Microsoft FUD announcements)

Can We Afford Not To Care?

  • Cannot afford ILS market trends
    • (How is your budget these days?)
  • Some users already seek componentized business processes
    • (Except they call them "mashups" and "web 2.0")
  • We need to move more nimbly ourselves
  • The major ILS products have long done an excellent job of automating how libraries deal with traditional resources. They have fallen behind, however, in dealing with electronic content.
    Marshall Breeding. "Musings on the State of the ILS in 2006" Computers in Libraries. 26(26): March 2006. pp 26-29.

The Alternatives

  • Reintegration:
    I'm concerned that the current path of relatively nonintegrated products will not be sustainable in the longer term. Would it be better if there were an ILS that would encompass a broader set of capabilities, including one for both print and electronic content, in a more integrated package?
    Marshall Breeding. "Musings on the State of the ILS in 2006"
  • Orchestrated Disintegration:
    SOA doesn't guarantee a happier, healthier life, free from business concerns. However, movement toward SOA is usually a movement toward freedom and flexibility and bodes well for the longevity of an organization and for the sanity of those usually held responsible.
    Judith Hurwitz, et al. Service Oriented Architecture for Dummies. Wiley Publishing, Inc.: Indianapolis, Indiana. 2006.

What Might a Library SOA Look Like?

Ground Rules

  • Service-orientation
  • Consistency and cohesiveness
  • Outward-facing
From Brian Lavoie, et al. "A Service Framework for Libraries"

The Business Processes of a Traditional ILS

  • Discovering Content
  • Describing Content
  • Borrowing Physical Item
  • Get New Content

Traditional and Non-traditional Services for Discovering Content

  • Search for Known Item (Author/Title/Subject searches plus OpenURL resolution)
  • Browse Related Items by Author/Series Title/Subject/Classification
  • Make Recommendations
  • Read Reviews
  • Browse Related Items using Relevance-Ranked Filters

Traditional and Non-traditional Services for Describing Content

  • Human-mediated description (a.k.a. "cataloging")
  • Automated Description Services
    • (a la Amazon's Statistically Improbable Phrases and Capitalized Phrases
    • ... Shape Recognition and Color Maps
    • ... Rhythm Indexes and Tonal Maps
    • ... Closed-Caption text parsing and automated scene change marking)
  • Record Annotation
    • (folksonomy tags,
    • user-supplied reviews,
    • blogging trackbacks)

Non-traditional Uses of Traditional ILS Functions

  • Discovering Content
  • Describing Content
  • Borrowing Physical Item
  • Get New Content
  • Institutional Repositories and Electronic Portfolios
  • Metasearch
  • E-learning and Course Management Systems
  • Making Content Discoverable Through External Agents

Non-Traditional Locations of Traditional and Non-Traditional ILS Functions

  • OCLC Search Results
  • Search Results with Inventory Status
  • Search Results with Useful Inventory Status...answering the "when can I have it?" question
  • Useful Inventory Status when you have inventory from more than one location

Who Else Is Talking About This? (Projects)

  • Digital Library Federation's Service Framework Group:
    Another possible end point of [the work of the DLF SFG] is the development of a service-oriented architecture for library systems. We are some way away from this as well; moreover the term "service-oriented architecture" is itself ill-defined and used inconsistently. However, one could imagine a service-oriented architecture for libraries as one that represented the library as a collection of autonomous yet linkable services, deployed and discoverable on the network through Web service technologies.
    Brian Lavoie, Geneva Henry, Lorcan Dempsey. "A Service Framework for Libraries" D-Lib Magazine. 12(7/8): July/August 2006. doi:10.1045/july2006-lavoie

Who Else Is Talking About This? (Projects)

  • California Digital Library's Common Framework:
    The CDL Common Framework is an open, services-oriented technical architecture that provides an integrating framework for services related to digital libraries. As a layered architecture it aims to separate front-end tools from back-end services from underlying data storage so that different components can be reused in multiple applications, reducing the time and money it takes to develop and maintain code. The Common Framework also supports easy integration of local and third party tools and services through a plug-in approach. It is available through both machine (i.e. web services) and human interfaces.

Who Else Is Talking About This? (Projects)

  • The eFramework for Education and Research:
    The eFramework for Education and Research; an overview briefing paper provides an overview of the e-Framework for Education and Research (the e-Framework), its strategic context, its aims and objectives, its overall approach, the stakeholders, the benefits and its expected outcomes and impact. Workflow and Webservices examines the topic of coordinating workflows of web services within an elearning context, and identifies the requirements, challenges, and technology choices involved. The JISC Information Environment (JISC IE) technical architecture specifies a set of standards and protocols that support the development and delivery of an integrated set of networked services that allow the end-user to discover, access, use and publish digital and physical resources as part of their learning and research activities.
